# AI Law Firm OS for Nigeria

PowerPoint-ready slide content for a first discussion with a Nigerian lawyer design partner.

---

## Slide 1: Title

**AI Law Firm OS for Nigeria**  
Lawyer-supervised legal operations for SMEs

**Subtitle**

Use AI to handle repetitive legal operations work while the lawyer stays in control of legal judgment, client advice, and final approval.

**Presenter notes**

This is not an AI lawyer. It is a legal operations platform that helps Nigerian lawyers and SMEs work faster, more consistently, and more profitably.

---

## Slide 2: The Problem

**Why this matters in Nigeria**

- Many Nigerian SMEs need help with business setup, compliance, contracts, and day-to-day legal operations.
- Lawyers spend too much time on repetitive work such as intake, checklists, draft preparation, reminders, and file organization.
- Clients often delay because they do not understand what documents are needed or what step comes next.
- Legal work becomes slow, fragmented, and hard to scale.

**Simple summary**

There is real demand, but the current process is too manual.

**Presenter notes**

The pain is not only lack of legal demand. The bigger problem is that small and mid-sized legal matters are operationally inefficient.

---

## Slide 3: Why Now

**Why this opportunity is timely**

- More SMEs want affordable and faster legal support.
- CAC registration and related startup workflows are already digitized, which makes legal operations easier to standardize.
- Compliance expectations are increasing around tax, privacy, employment, and recordkeeping.
- AI can now handle repetitive operational work like drafting, extraction, organization, reminders, and issue spotting.

**Simple summary**

The market is ready for a lawyer-supervised digital legal operations platform.

**Presenter notes**

The opportunity is not in replacing lawyers. It is in making lawyers more productive and making legal support easier for SMEs to buy.

---

## Slide 4: The Solution

**What we are building**

- A Nigeria-first legal operations platform
- AI handles repetitive workflow tasks
- A real lawyer reviews and approves high-risk outputs
- The system tracks matters, tasks, drafts, approvals, and documents in one place

**Core positioning**

AI does the repetitive work.  
The lawyer stays in control of legal judgment.

**Presenter notes**

This creates a safer and more credible product than a generic legal chatbot.

---

## Slide 5: What the App Can Do

**Current product capabilities**

- Collect founder or client information through structured intake
- Create a tracked legal matter
- Generate workflow steps and document briefs
- Produce source-backed draft answers for narrow legal operations questions
- Route high-risk work to lawyer review
- Track approvals, document versions, and matter history

**In plain language**

The app works like a smart legal assistant plus a matter management system.

**Presenter notes**

The value is speed, structure, repeatability, and control.

---

## Slide 6: What the App Does Step by Step

**How a matter flows**

1. Client or founder submits information
2. The app organizes the matter and identifies the workflow
3. AI drafts checklists, first-pass documents, and issue lists
4. The system retrieves source-backed material for narrow questions
5. The lawyer reviews, edits, approves, or rejects
6. Final next steps are sent to the client

**Simple summary**

This turns messy legal requests into a repeatable workflow.

**Presenter notes**

The main advantage is that the lawyer no longer starts from a blank page every time.

---

## Slide 7: First Use Cases

**Best Nigeria-first workflows**

- Business incorporation plus post-incorporation compliance
- First employee onboarding pack
- Vendor or service agreement workflow
- Privacy and data-handling checklist
- Compliance reminders and annual filing support

**Recommended first pilot**

Business incorporation plus post-incorporation compliance

**Presenter notes**

This is the strongest starting point because it is common, structured, and easy to monetize beyond one-time setup.

## Slide 8: What the Lawyer Still Controls

**The lawyer remains responsible for**

- final review
- client-facing advice
- approval of filings and contract language
- escalation of high-risk issues

**The AI is used for**

- intake
- drafting
- organization
- checklists
- reminders
- issue spotting

**Simple summary**

The platform supports the lawyer. It does not replace the lawyer.

**Presenter notes**

This point is critical for trust, compliance, and adoption.

**Why this product is different**

This is a legal operations system, not just a chat interface.

**Revenue streams**

- per-document or per-workflow fees

**Examples**

## Slide 12: Best Initial Monetization Model

**Start with this structure**

- one-time setup fee for business launch
- recurring compliance plan after setup
- extra fees for document workflows and lawyer escalation

**Suggested structure**

- Incorporation and compliance setup
- Monthly compliance and reminder plan
- Add-on document packs
- Paid lawyer review for high-risk matters

**Simple summary**

Use incorporation as the entry point, then convert clients into recurring compliance customers.

**Presenter notes**

This is how the product becomes a real business instead of a one-off service.

---

## Slide 13: Why Start With Nigeria

**Why Nigeria first**

- one legal environment to master first
- one regulator and workflow ecosystem at a time
- easier source curation
- easier lawyer review design
- easier trust building with local clients

**Why not all of Africa immediately**

- every country has different laws, regulators, filings, templates, and compliance rules
- trying to cover everything at once weakens quality and trust

**Simple summary**

Win Nigeria first. Expand later using country packs.

**Presenter notes**

This is the right strategy operationally and commercially.

---

## Slide 14: How We Expand Across Africa

**Expansion model**

- One platform
- Multiple jurisdiction packs

**Each country pack includes**

- local laws and regulatory sources
- local workflows and compliance rules
- local templates
- local lawyer review policies
- local partner network

**Sequence**

1. Nigeria
2. prove product-market fit
3. replicate country by country

**Presenter notes**

The company vision can be Africa-wide, but the execution should stay country-specific.

---

## Slide 15: Pilot Proposal

**Pilot structure**

- Duration: 30 days
- Scope: 5 to 10 real SME matters
- Focus: one workflow only
- Review: lawyer-supervised outputs
- Goal: validate speed, quality, and revenue potential

**Recommended first workflow**

Business incorporation plus post-incorporation compliance

**Presenter notes**

The pilot should be practical and measurable, not theoretical.

---
